What ISR is

ISR (Infant Swimming Resource) is an international organization dedicated to aquatic self-rescue for infants and young children. The ISR Self-Rescue program combines behavioral science with pediatric health guidance so lessons match how babies learn. Only licensed ISR instructors may teach this curriculum; it is not a traditional group swim class.

One global standard

ISR publishes a single, evidence-informed protocol used by instructors across the country and abroad.

Deep instructor training

Certified instructors complete intensive in-water training, exams, and ongoing renewal to keep their license active.

Skills for real emergencies

The focus is survival: floating, breath control, and recovery when a child reaches the water alone.

WaveFront is an independent provider of the official ISR Self-Rescue program.
