Core Programs

Foundational skills that stay with your child for a lifetime.

ISR Self-Rescue® Infant

Children 6 months – 12 months

Duration: 6 – 7 weeks

Children learn the ISR Self-Rescue skill of rolling onto their backs to float, rest, and breathe. They learn to maintain this position until help arrives. This is the initial ISR course for infants—one-on-one lessons tailored to your baby’s development.

ISR Self-Rescue® Toddler

Children 1 year – 6 years

Duration: 2 – 4 weeks

Building on solid fundamentals, toddlers learn the Swim-Float-Swim sequence, how to reach the pool edge or steps safely, and how to perform skills in clothing for real-world readiness—always with focused, one-on-one instruction.

Common Questions

Everything you need to know before your first splash.

Why is the program 5 days a week? expand_more

Consistency is key for infant muscle memory. Short, daily exposures help build skills faster and safer than longer sessions once a week.

How long are the actual lessons? expand_more

Lessons are 10 minutes long. This is the optimal window for focused learning in young children without physical or mental fatigue.

What if my child cries? expand_more

Crying is a natural form of communication. Our instructor is trained to nurture and build trust, turning tears into triumphant smiles over time.
